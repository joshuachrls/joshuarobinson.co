About Me

Would we be a good match? With every new project I attempt to pull problems into the present and resolve them. This is that value in action. I’m eager to tackle design problems that are:

  • ambitious

    Your product or service will scale. There are opportunities at each level to evaluate and maximize.

  • human-centered

    This is not just a buzzword. Your product or service could make the world a better place. I don’t have to advocate for the user because user-centered design is inherent in the product itself.

  • growth

    I’m relatively new to UX/UI design and front-end development. I believe the only capstone to the skills I can learn is my own desire to learn them.

Process

Everything begins with words. Even before I start sketching, I write. Writing helps me focus on transforming abstractions into concrete ideas. This is how I identify the core value of a product for users–the edges and depth of the design. I examine my language and I make sure it reflects shared meanings and definitions across project stakeholders and the design team. Writing and research provide the answer to a fundamental question: What objects will allow me to compare my mental model with others? Sometimes the answer is a sitemap, a wireframe, a Gantt chart or a heuristic evaluation–or all of them. I ask myself at each juncture: What do people need to understand?

For high-fidelity wireframes I usually use Bohemian Coding’s Sketch because it’s precise, fast and relatively distraction-free. When I need expert control, Adobe Illustrator or Photoshop–though weighted down by legacy–are still unrivaled. Omnigraffle is also very versatile for certain types of documentation. For prototyping designs, I have used Proto.io, UxPin and Invision.

It's great that new tools like Macaw and Blocs allow me to mockup an entire website without coding. However, learning to code semantic HTML and CSS has made me a better designer, hands down. When you are forced to search for solutions from a different perspective, you enhance your capacity for understanding the big picture. I’m a huge advocate for learning code. I have studied Javascript and jQuery and I can use them on a website, though I am far from fluent. I’m can use CSS pre-processors like Saas and HTML boilerplates like Bootstrap and Foundation. I also keep up with newer frameworks such as PatternLab and SpaceBase.
