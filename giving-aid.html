GivingAid.org

GivingAid is an organization that raises money and awareness for Chicago non-profits. My team was asked to explore three new strategies for raising money for charity and also design a landing page presenting the concept.

  • Research
  • Concept
  • Interface Design
  • Copywriting

Goals

In the real world of action, good intentions tend to fail. Intuitively, people want to be charitable, but the deliberate part of their mind stumbles over the pain points of giving. I saw a chance for GivingAid to remove some of these pain points: finding trusted charities, knowing when to give, and tracking donations.

GivingAid asks users to subscribe for $5 a month, effectively establishing a routine that doesn’t require conscious thought about when and how to give to charity. GivingAid disperses the $5 equally among trusted local charities that have been vetted. I wanted GivingAid to eliminate all of the work required of the user beyond giving initial consent. I chose $5 as the monthly donation amount because it is less than what people typically associate with monthly fees. Netflix, for example, is 7.99 or 9.99 a month, but you receive ongoing rewards (access to the service) throughout the month. Since GivingAid only offers an abstract reward (the feeling of being charitable) it was important the subscription fee seem disposable, like a cup of coffee.

Design Process

Every concept and design begins with words. After figuring out what I wanted to accomplish, I began sketching some ideas. Since GivingAid is based in Chicago, I chose colors that hint at Chicago’s flag. An inspiring hero image evokes a sense of community. To contrast with the dark hero image, I used bright illustrations: one is Chicago’s iconic Willis Tower and the other is a match with the title, “Light a Spark.”
