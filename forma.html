FORMA

FORMA was founded on the desire to motivate and foster community across all artistic disciplines. As an online publication, it celebrated the influence of innovative founders and told the stories of those creating new paths in the disciplines of architecture, design, literature, photography, food, fashion and business.

Goals

I founded FORMA in 2012 and produced four issues in collaboration with photographers and writers from all parts of the country. Over the course of four issues we grew to an online readership of more than 25,000. The idea behind FORMA stems from my observation of three creative constructs: Space, Form, and Ethic. Taken together, the complex and varied interplay between these constructs enhances creative output, affects clarity, and helps us engage our community. These became the common theme and foundation for every story in FORMA.

Three Constructs

  • Space / context affects content

    • Space is the environment we inhabit–where we work and create. It is visual, acoustic, communal, psychosocial and interactive. As a foundational concept in design, space is how objects are perceived in relation to their proximity to one another. In acoustic sound theory it is all the frequencies, carved and tuned to make room for one another. The clarity of a certain tone might elicit an emotional response because competing frequencies are purposely filtered to enhance or make room for the tone. In this construct lives all the beauty of architecture, interior design, acoustic sound theory, recording studios, live music and theater.

  • Form / the signal transmitting the message

    • Form is physical and touch. It's the signal transmitting the message of the ethic. It's consumable and purposeful like product design, food, drink, furniture and fashion. Form is the consumer-facing construct.

  • Ethic / the message in the signal

    • Ethic is inherent in every work of original creation. Creativity does not exist in a vacuum. Ethic attempts to understand the story behind the form. It explores the tension often found between Space and Form and asks how someone’s story exerts influence over their creativity. In user-centered design thinking, the ethic would be the desire of the designer to help the user accomplish their goals.
